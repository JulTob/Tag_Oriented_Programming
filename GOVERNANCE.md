# Governance

Tag-Oriented Programming is led by a **Director** — authority held in trust for the community, inside a structure built to grow.

## The Director

**Julio Toboso (@JulTob)** is the Director of Tag-Oriented Programming: the steward and, for now, the final design authority over the Specification. The Director sets the direction and keeps the paradigm coherent while it is young — the way the head of an agency sets the mission its Agents carry out.

This is a **Bastón de Mando**, not a throne — a *staff of command* **held in trust** for the community. (English has no exact word for it; "staff of command" or "stewardship" come closest.) It is held to *organize and serve*, not to own. The Director is the first to answer for TOP's coherence — and the first to hand off responsibility as the project earns it.

## Growth path

The role is built to broaden:

- As contributors prove out a track, the Director appoints **Section Chiefs** — trusted leads for a track (the Specification, a language implementation, the process itself).
- A **Council** of Section Chiefs advises and, over time, shares decisions, with the Director as first among equals rather than sole decider.
- Trademark and final authority may pass to a neutral body if and when the project outgrows a single steward.

Until then the Director holds the staff — to keep one source of truth, and protect it from fragmenting before it can stand on its own.

## How decisions are made

Changes move through the **STEP** process — see [`steps/README.md`](steps/README.md) and [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md). Every STEP records its status and the reason it was **Cleared** or **Redacted**, in the open. Small fixes go through ordinary issues and PRs; all merges to `main` require the Director's review (`CODEOWNERS`).
